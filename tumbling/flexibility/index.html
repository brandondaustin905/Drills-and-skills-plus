Flexibility
& Range

Not about looking bendy. About being able to hit a shape without your lower back paying for it.

⏱ 7 min read🧘 Daily-ish🏠 Home friendly

One honest thing first

Stretching gets sold as injury prevention. The evidence for that is weak — a panel of international stretching researchers recently reached agreement that they don't recommend stretching for injury prevention in general, and systematic reviews have repeatedly found no meaningful drop in overall injury rates.

What does reduce injury risk is strength. That evidence is much stronger.

So: this lesson unlocks the shapes. Your conditioning keeps you safe. You need both, and they're doing different jobs.

The open shoulder line

The single most important range in tumbling, and the one most often missing.

Hinge, don't curve

Range should come from the big joints — hips and shoulders — not from rounding the spine.

When to stretch what

This one surprises most athletes: long static holds can temporarily reduce power. Doing them right before explosive tumbling works against you.

BeforeDynamic — leg swings, lunges, circles
TumblingSkip the long holds
AfterStatic holds, 20–30s

Range work sticks better in a separate session or after training anyway. Warm tissue lengthens; cold tissue resists and fights back.

1
BHS · Layout

Shoulder bridge

From a bridge, actively drive the chest forward over the hands, working toward stacking the shoulders directly over the wrists. Feet stay glued to the floor.

Watch forIf the shoulders won't travel, don't force it by arching harder. Lower the height, take the range you have.
2
Tucks · Aerials

Hip flexor lunge

Deep lunge, front knee stacked precisely over the ankle. Pelvis tucked under, torso completely vertical. Drive the hips forward slightly without letting the lower back arch.

Watch forThe lower back arching is the cheat. Tuck the pelvis harder and the stretch moves to where it belongs.
3
Walkovers · Layout

Flat-back pike fold

Standing or seated, pull the kneecaps up to engage the quads. Hinge strictly from the hip joints, spine completely flat and neutral. Reach the chest toward the toes.

Watch forDropping the head to the knees. That's a rounded spine, not a hamstring stretch — and it feels deeper while doing less.

Safe conditioning protocol

Warm first. 10–15 minutes raising body temperature. Cold tissue doesn't lengthen, it resists.

Never force it. Push into a stretch and the muscle protectively contracts — you get the opposite of what you came for. Controlled and progressive, always.

No bouncing, no partner pushing you deeper. Range built by force comes off just as fast and takes something with it.

Pair it with strength. Unlocked range without the strength to stabilise it mid-air is useless and genuinely dangerous.

Sharp pinching in a joint, lower back pain, or anything down the back of the leg is a stop-and-report — not something to breathe through.
