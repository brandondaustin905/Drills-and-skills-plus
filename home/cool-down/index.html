Cool-Down
Flexibility

Seven stretches, warm muscles, calm breathing. Relaxation into range — not forcing it.

⏱ ~8 min🔥 After training only🎧 Guided mode

This is how every session ends

Whichever drill you just did — motions, jumps, core, anything — this is the finish.

Guided cool-down

Every stretch timed, both sides called, no stopwatch needed. Sit down and follow along.

After training only

Static stretching — holding a position for 20–30 seconds — is ideal at the end of a session, on warm muscle. It's the wrong tool before explosive work, where long holds can temporarily reduce your power.

What this actually does

Flexibility is part of performance, not separate from it. Tight muscles limit jump extension, toe point clarity, tumbling shapes and stunt lines.

One honest note: stretching gets sold as injury prevention, and the evidence there is weaker than people assume — strength training carries the stronger case. So treat this as building and maintaining the range your skills need, and as a calm end to a session. Not as armour.

1
Hamstrings

Pike stretch

Sit with legs straight together, toes flexed toward you. Reach toward your feet or shins, moving your chest toward your thighs.

What to feelHamstrings lengthening · spine gently folding · controlled release down the back of the legs
ErrorsRounding the back with no intention → think chest to legs · Locked knees but tense legs → relax into it · Bouncing → hold steady and breathe
Beginner
Slight knee bend.
Advanced
Reach past the feet with a flat back before folding deeper.
2
Inner thighs

Straddle stretch

Sit with legs wide in a V. Toes pointed or flexed, whichever is comfortable. Slowly reach forward toward the floor.

What to feelInner thighs opening · a deep groin stretch · a long line through both legs
ErrorsCollapsing the chest forward without control · Knees bending inward · Overreaching with no breath control
Beginner
Smaller straddle width.
Advanced
Chest closer to the floor, or alternating reaches to each leg.
3
Hips & groin

Butterfly stretch

Sit tall. Soles of the feet together, holding your ankles or feet. Gently press the knees toward the floor.

What to feelInner thigh release · hips opening · relaxation through the groin
ErrorsForcing the knees down · Rounded, collapsed posture · Tension instead of relaxation
Beginner
Sit on a raised surface.
Advanced
Lean forward with a flat back over the feet.
4
Both sides

Hip flexor stretch

Lunge, back knee on the ground, front knee stacked over the ankle. Tuck the pelvis under, then gently push the hips forward.

What to feelThe front of the hip opening on the back leg · length through the hip flexor and quad
ErrorsArching the lower back instead of shifting the hips · Front knee drifting past the toes · Overstretching too quickly
Beginner
Reduce the lunge depth.
Advanced
Raise the back arm overhead for a longer line.
5
Both sides

Hamstring stretch

Sitting or standing, extend one leg forward with toes pointed up. Reach toward the foot while keeping your spine controlled.

What to feelBack of the thigh lengthening · controlled stretch through the posterior chain
ErrorsRounded back collapse · Locking into pain instead of stretch · Bouncing or pulsing
Beginner
Slight knee bend.
Advanced
Deeper reach with a flat back first.
6
Both sides

Calf stretch

Stand facing a wall. One leg forward and bent, back leg straight. Press the back heel into the floor and lean forward gently.

What to feelDeep calf and Achilles stretch · heel grounded and stable
ErrorsBack heel lifting · Too much upper body force · Bent back knee
Beginner
Smaller stance.
Advanced
Deeper lean.
7
Both sides

Shoulder stretch

Bring one arm across your body. Use the opposite arm to gently pull it closer. Keep your shoulders down and relaxed.

What to feelRear shoulder and upper back release · controlled tension, not strain
ErrorsShrugging the shoulders up · Pulling too aggressively · Holding your breath
Beginner
Light pressure only.
Advanced
Hold slightly longer, staying relaxed.

Add these for your position

Different roles load different areas. Give these extra attention.

Bases & back spotsLow back and wrists take the most load in this role. Add gentle wrist circles and wrist flexor stretches, plus a knees-to-chest or gentle spinal twist on your back.
FlyersHips, hamstrings and shoulders carry the demands of body positions. The straddle, butterfly and hip flexor stretches above matter most for you — give them the full time.
TumblersWrists, shoulders and calves absorb the impact. Wrist stretches and the calf stretch are worth extra attention.

Safety

Never bounce, never force. Range built by force comes off just as fast.

Tension, never pain. If it's sharp, stop.

No partner pushing you deeper. The pressure comes from you, so you control it.

Breathe throughout. Holding your breath is a sign you've gone too far.

If you're still growing, keep it gentle and consistent rather than aggressive. Progress here is measured in months.
