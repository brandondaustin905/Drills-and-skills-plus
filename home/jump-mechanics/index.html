Guided drill

Paces every phase of every slow rep — prep, swing, shape, land — then counts your fast reps. Phone down, follow the calls.

Why this order matters

Your nervous system builds movement pathways through repetition. Slow practice builds the pattern. Fast practice tests it.

Which means speed doesn't fix a sloppy jump — it magnifies it. You're not just performing badly, you're rehearsing badly, and the mistake gets wired in deeper every rep.

The four positions

Every rep, slow or fast, passes through these. Learn them here.

The shape, right and wrong

The hip cueHips rotate back, so kneecaps and shoelaces point up at the ceiling. If they roll forward, the legs come up in front and the chest follows them down.
Phase 1 · Slow

10 slow motion reps

Purpose: awareness. You're teaching your body what "correct" feels like. Slow reps aren't easy — they expose weaknesses.

What "slow motion" meansA small jump, or no jump at all. You're walking through the pattern deliberately and hitting every position. Don't chase height in this phase.
1
Strong prepDip with control — knees bend, chest stays lifted. Do not collapse forward.
2
Arm swingFrom your sides, back, then drive up. Even slowly, the path stays sharp and intentional.
3
Jump & shapeLegs straight, toes pointed, hips rotated back, core tight, chest controlled. Arms in a T.
4
Controlled landingQuiet. Knees bend softly, chest up, balance held. Reset fully between reps.
Watch forRushing instead of controlling · arms drifting or losing tension · breaking at the hips · landing heavy or collapsing forward
"Slow is strong." · "Every position is intentional." · "Tight body, clean lines."
Phase 2 · Fast

10 full-speed reps

Purpose: transfer control into power. This is where athletes lose technique — your job is protecting what you just built.

1
Strong prepDip quickly, stay controlled, no collapsing.
2
Fast arm driveThis is your engine. Fast, sharp, fully extended. No lazy arms, no half swings.
3
Explode & hitPush aggressively through the floor. Legs straight immediately, core tight, clean position. Maximum effort without losing shape.
4
Soft landingQuiet feet, soft knees, stable chest. Every landing should look like you could go again instantly.
Watch forChaos in pursuit of height · bent knees in the air · weak or delayed arm swing · loud heavy landings · losing the toe point under speed
"Explode up, freeze clean." · "Clean first, then powerful." · "Stick every landing like it matters."
