Sharp
Motions

Judges don't just see what you do — they score how you do it. Eight motions, hit clean and held.

⏱ ~10 min🪞 Mirror helps🎧 Guided mode

Guided drill

Each motion called and timed, with a mini-diagram of the shape to hit. Mirror in front of you, phone to the side.

The whole idea

Sharp motions aren't about speed. They're about intention — snapping to an exact target and stopping there, dead.

The single biggest tell between a beginner and a clean athlete: beginners place a motion, drifting into position. Strong athletes hit it — arms locked to target as fast as they can, then still.

The eight motions

Every one starts and ends in Clean. Hold each 3–5 seconds. Fists tight unless noted.

High VUp & out, 45°. Thumbs forward.
Low VThe exact inverse of High V.
TStraight out, one line, shoulder height.
Broken TT, then forearms fold in. Elbows stay up.
TouchdownStraight up, arms brushing the ears.
DaggersFists point down at the chest, elbows tight.
CandlesticksOut front, grip circles facing up.
CleanStraight down, tight to the sides.
The sequenceHigh V → Low V → T → Broken T → Touchdown → Daggers → Candlesticks → Clean. Run it 3× · rest 30–45s between rounds.

Peripheral vision test

For High V and T, there's a built-in self-check: you should just barely see both arms out of the corners of your eyes.

Check every motion

  • Elbows locked straight (where they should be)
  • Fists tight, wrists firm
  • Shoulders down and relaxed
  • Core tight, standing tall
  • Both arms symmetrical
  • Each motion stopped with control
Common mistakesBent elbows · loose wrists or open hands · raised shoulders · leaning forward or back · relaxed core · rushing between motions · placing instead of hitting

Cool down

Light stretches for shoulders, chest, upper back and wrists. A few deep breaths, and reset your posture.
